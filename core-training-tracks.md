## Builder Track

**Prerequisites (Safety & Setup – Do This First)**
Before any cutting or building:

- List and identify all basic tools (tape measure, speed square, pencil, handsaw or circular saw, hammer, drill/driver, clamps, level, safety gear).
- **Mandatory safety setup**: Safety glasses, hearing protection (for power tools), dust mask, sturdy closed-toe shoes, no loose clothing/jewelry, tied-back hair. Clear workspace, good lighting, no trip hazards. Never wear gloves with spinning tools.
- **Copilot prompt**: "Describe your workspace and list your tools + safety gear. Send a photo of your setup."
- **Feedback**: Correct any missing safety items immediately. Assign a "safety reinforcement task" (e.g., practice tool storage and workspace sweep) if needed.

**Progress signal**: User demonstrates safe setup → Unlock Level 1.

**Level 1 – Basic Actions (Tool Familiarity & Fundamentals)**
Focus: Use tools safely, understand materials, follow simple instructions. Build muscle memory and accuracy.

Tasks (do in order, repeat as needed for consistency):

1. **Measure & Mark Accurately**
   - Task: Measure and clearly mark 10 different lengths on scrap wood (mix of 6", 12", 24", etc.) using tape measure and pencil. Include inside/outside measurements.
   - Success criteria: All marks within ±1/16 inch when double-checked.
   - Just-in-time: "Measure twice, cut once"; how to read tape accurately; hook placement.
   - Feedback: "What did you do? Any hesitation? Send photo of marked pieces with tape measure showing accuracy." Identify errors (parallax, wrong hook use) → assign correction (remark 5 more).

2. **Identify & Handle Materials**
   - Task: Sort 5+ pieces of scrap wood by type, check for defects (warps, knots, cracks), mark grain direction.
   - Success: Correct identification + explanation of usability.
   - Error use: Warped piece? Explain wood movement.

3. **Straight Cuts – Hand or Power Saw**
   - Task: Make 5 straight cross-cuts on scrap 1x4 or 2x4, following marked lines.
   - Success: Cuts within ±1/8 inch of line, square (gap <1/16" with speed square), no binding.
   - Common errors: Wrong side of line (kerf loss), dull blade, rushing, poor support.
   - Repeat until 3 consecutive good cuts.

**Progress gate to Level 2**: 3 identical boards cut to same length within ±1/16" accuracy. User sends photos + measurements. Copilot: "Ready for controlled builds" or "Reinforce measuring/cutting."
**Repeatability check**: Can user do the task again without guidance?

**Level 2 – Controlled Builds (Small, Defined Tasks – Repeat for Consistency)**
Focus: Assemble simple items. Emphasize squareness, stability, and repeatability.

1. **Build a Simple Box or Tray (Butt Joints)**
   - Task: Cut 4 sides + bottom to make a small open box (e.g., 8"x8"x4" inside). Use butt joints, screws or nails.
   - Materials: Scrap 1x4/1x6 + plywood/thin board for bottom.
   - Success: Square (diagonals equal within 1/8"), stable, no wobble, joints tight.
   - Feedback: "Show assembled box from multiple angles + diagonals measured. What went wrong?"
   - Errors → corrections: Gaps (clamping/screw placement), crooked assembly (use square), weak joints (pre-drill).

2. **Repeat the Box 2–3 Times** with slight variations (different sizes or added divider).
   Goal: Consistent results with minimal help.

**Progress gate**: 2–3 repeatable square and stable boxes. User explains improvements. Copilot validates via photos/descriptions.
**Just-in-time**: Basic joinery, clamping, pre-drilling.

**Level 3 – Real-World Tasks (Fix or Build Something Needed)**
Focus: Apply skills to actual useful items. Push toward home/community impact.

Tasks (copilot assigns based on environment):

1. **Repair a Broken Item**
   - Examples: Loose drawer, sagging shelf, broken chair leg, door that won't close.
   - Task: Diagnose, disassemble if needed, repair/replace, reassemble.
   - Success: Item functions better, repair stable and neat.
   - Feedback: Before/after photos + diagnosis/fix description. "Where did you hesitate?"

2. **Build a Usable Shelf or Storage Unit**
   - Task: Build simple wall-mounted or freestanding shelf (e.g., 3-shelf ~24–36" wide) for books/tools/food.
   - Success: Level and plumb, holds weight without sagging, securely mounted.
   - Real integration: "This will go in your kitchen/storage area – measure the space first."

**Progress gate**: At least one repair + one new build, verified useful. User reports real outcome ("It now holds X lbs without issue").
**Just-in-time** (as needed): Finding studs, basic bracing.

**Level 4 – Diagnosis (Identify Problems, Choose Solutions)**
Focus: Judgment and troubleshooting.

1. **Analyze a Failed or Problematic Structure**
   - Task: Find unstable/crooked/broken item. Diagnose root cause (poor joinery, no support, wrong fasteners, wood movement). Propose and execute fix.
   - Success: Accurate diagnosis + successful correction. Explain trade-offs.
   - Feedback: Detailed before/during/after photos. Probe: "What failed and why? Alternative solutions?"

2. **Plan a Small Multi-Part Build with Diagnosis**
   - Example: Build small workbench or sawhorse sturdy and level on uneven ground. Identify failure points in advance.
   - Success: Build works; user anticipates and mitigates issues.

**Progress gate**: Consistent ability to spot/fix problems independently. Copilot: "Needs reinforcement in X" or "Ready for independence."
**Error-first**: Surface past mistakes (ignoring grain, poor clamping, wrong fasteners) as teaching moments.

**Level 5 – Independence (Plan, Execute, Adjust Without Guidance)**
Focus: User leads. Copilot shifts to reviewer/consultant.

- User proposes real community/home project (e.g., storage cabinet, gate repair, benches, raised garden bed frame).
- Requirements: Detailed plan (materials list, cut list, steps), safety considerations, timeline.
- Execute → document process/outcome.
- Success: Project completed with minimal input, meets needs, professional enough for community use. Adjustable for real issues (material shortages, site problems).
- Feedback: Post-project review only. Track independence metric.

**Progress signal**: "Ready to be assigned real community tasks" or "Can now evaluate Level 1–3 apprentices with oversight."
**Output**: Hand-off readiness score (e.g., 80%+ independence, repeatable quality).

## Overall Track Mechanics for the Copilot

- After every task: Ask: "What did you do? What went wrong? Show result (photo + measurements/descriptions). Where did you hesitate?"
- Then: Identify specific mistakes → explain only what's needed → assign targeted correction task.
- **Progress tracking**: Tasks completed, repeatability (3x consistent), independence level, problem-solving demonstrated.
- **Advancement**: Strict — only move up on proven results. Loop back with variations if stuck.
- **Real-world push**: Every Level 3+ task aims for immediate community value (fix shared item, build for storage/food/repair).
- **Failure library**: Log anonymized mistakes (e.g., "crooked cut due to unsupported workpiece") for later users.
- **Repeatability gates**: Between levels, require 2–3 identical successful executions.

This track is self-contained yet expandable. It starts extremely concrete and scales to judgment/independence exactly as your original vision requires. It avoids vague theory — every step centers on doing real work.

## Water Systems Track

**Goal**: Turn a beginner into a reliable operator who can collect, store, purify, distribute, maintain, and reuse water in a home or community setting — with immediate practical value (emergency storage, rainwater harvesting, greywater for irrigation, basic repairs). Emphasizes safety, cleanliness, and preventing contamination.

**Important Safety Note** (built into the copilot): Water systems involve contamination risks, pressure, and electricity (if pumps involved). Always prioritize potable vs. non-potable separation. Use food-grade materials only. Test water quality when possible (TDS meter, visual clarity, smell). Never assume water is safe to drink without proper treatment. Local codes/permitting may apply for greywater or large systems.

## Prerequisites (Safety & Setup – Do This First)

Before any water handling:

- Identify and list tools/materials: tape measure, pipe cutter or hacksaw, adjustable wrench, channel-lock pliers, Teflon tape, plumber’s putty or sealant, buckets, funnel, food-grade hoses/containers, level, gloves, safety glasses, cleaning supplies (bleach or approved sanitizer).
- **Mandatory safety & hygiene setup**: Clean workspace, hand-washing station, separate tools for potable vs. non-potable work if possible. Understand basic contamination risks (cross-connection, standing water breeding bacteria).
- **Task**: Describe your current water sources (municipal, well, rainwater potential) and list available containers/tools. Send photo of workspace and safety setup.
- **Feedback**: Correct missing items immediately. Assign reinforcement (e.g., practice sanitizing containers) if needed.

**Progress signal**: Safe, hygienic setup demonstrated → Unlock Level 1.

## Level 1 – Basic Actions (Tool Familiarity, Materials, Simple Handling)

Focus: Safe handling, measurement, basic assembly/disassembly, understanding materials and contamination risks.

**Tasks** (in order, repeat for consistency):

1. **Measure, Cut & Connect Basic Pipes/Hoses**
   - Task: Measure and cut 5 short lengths of hose or PVC pipe (e.g., 12–24" pieces). Practice making secure, leak-free connections using hose clamps, threaded fittings, or basic couplings. Test with water (non-potable first).
   - Success criteria: Cuts straight and square; connections hold without leaks under gentle pressure; repeatable within tolerances.
   - Just-in-time: How to measure pipe (account for fitting depth), proper Teflon tape direction, avoiding overtightening.
   - Feedback: "What did you do? Any leaks or hesitation? Send photos of cuts + assembled/tested pieces with water test." Common errors: crooked cuts, wrong tape direction, loose clamps → targeted correction tasks.

2. **Identify & Prepare Containers/Materials**
   - Task: Select and sanitize 3+ food-grade containers (buckets, jugs, barrels). Check for defects (cracks, old residues). Label clearly (potable vs. non-potable).
   - Success: Proper identification + sanitization (e.g., bleach solution rinse, rinse thoroughly). Explain why certain plastics are safe/unsafe.
   - Error use: Residue left? Teach thorough cleaning protocols.

3. **Basic Water Transfer & Measurement**
   - Task: Measure and transfer exact volumes (e.g., 1 gallon, 5 gallons) between containers using buckets/hoses. Practice siphoning or pouring without spillage or contamination.
   - Success: Accurate volumes (within 5–10% visually or with measured marks), no cross-contamination.

**Progress gate to Level 2**: 3 repeatable leak-free connections + sanitized containers verified via photos/descriptions. Copilot: "Ready for controlled systems" or "Reinforce cutting/sealing."
**Repeatability check**: User completes similar task again with minimal prompting.

## Level 2 – Controlled Builds (Small, Defined Assemblies – Repeat for Consistency)

Focus: Assemble simple functional components. Emphasize sealing, stability, and basic flow control.

**Tasks**:

1. **Build a Simple Rainwater Collection Barrel Setup**
   - Task: Set up one 55-gallon (or similar) food-grade barrel with inlet (from downspout or hose), overflow, and spigot/outlet. Include basic screen/filter at inlet. Position level and stable.
   - Materials: Barrel, spigot kit, screen/mesh, basic fittings.
   - Success: No leaks at connections, overflow works, barrel stable and level. Test with water (non-potable).
   - Feedback: Multiple-angle photos + description of assembly and water test. "What went wrong?"
   - Common errors: Poor sealing (leaks), unstable base, inlet clogging → corrections on clamping, leveling, screening.

2. **Repeat with Variations**
   - Add a first-flush diverter or connect two barrels in series. Goal: Consistent, repeatable results with less guidance.

**Progress gate**: 2–3 successful barrel setups (or equivalent) that function without leaks. User explains improvements made.
**Just-in-time**: Basic plumbing principles (gravity flow, avoiding air locks), food-grade requirements, simple filtration layering.

## Level 3 – Real-World Tasks (Fix or Build Something Needed)

Focus: Apply skills to actual useful water systems in the home/community. Immediate value creation.

**Tasks** (copilot assigns based on user environment/resources):

1. **Repair a Leaking or Inefficient Water Component**
   - Examples: Fix a dripping faucet, leaking hose connection, clogged gutter/downspout, or failing seal on existing storage.
   - Task: Diagnose, disassemble if needed, repair/replace, reassemble and test.
   - Success: Issue resolved, system performs better, no new leaks. Before/after photos + function test.

2. **Build/Install a Useful Water Feature**
   - Examples: Full home rainwater barrel system with overflow; simple greywater diversion from laundry or shower to garden irrigation; basic elevated storage with gravity feed.
   - Success: System collects/stores/distributes water effectively, integrates safely (no backflow risk), provides measurable benefit (e.g., X gallons captured).
   - Real integration: "This setup will support your garden or emergency storage — measure your roof/gutter area first."

**Progress gate**: At least one repair + one new build completed and verified in use. User reports real outcome (e.g., "Collected 40 gallons during last rain, no leaks").
**Just-in-time** (as needed): Greywater safety, basic backflow prevention, mounting/securing tanks.

## Level 4 – Diagnosis (Identify Problems, Choose Solutions)

Focus: Troubleshooting judgment over rote execution.

**Tasks**:

1. **Analyze and Fix a Problematic Water System**
   - Task: Find or simulate an issue (low flow, contamination signs, leaks, poor collection). Diagnose root cause (clogged filter? Wrong slope? Contaminated container? Loose fitting?). Execute targeted fix.
   - Success: Accurate diagnosis + permanent resolution. Explain alternatives and why chosen.
   - Feedback: Detailed before/during/after description + photos. Probe: "What failed and why? What other solutions exist?"

2. **Plan a Multi-Component System with Risk Assessment**
   - Example: Design a small integrated setup (collection + storage + basic filtration + distribution) for a specific need. Identify failure points in advance (freezing, algae, pressure loss). Build and test.

**Progress gate**: Consistent diagnosis and fixes without major guidance. Copilot flags "Needs reinforcement in contamination control" or similar.
**Error-first**: Common mistakes (improper layering, forgetting to sanitize, overtightening, ignoring slope) become targeted lessons.

## Level 5 – Independence (Plan, Execute, Adjust Without Guidance)

Focus: User drives real projects. Copilot acts as reviewer/consultant only.

**Tasks**:

- User proposes a practical community/home water project (e.g., full rainwater harvesting with filtration, greywater irrigation system, expanded storage, or seasonal maintenance plan).
- Requirements: Detailed plan (materials list, cut/fit list, steps, safety/contamination controls, estimated yield), risk assessment.
- Execute, document process and outcomes, adjust for real issues (leaks, material limits, weather).
- Success: Project delivers reliable water function, meets needs safely, with minimal copilot input. Scalable and maintainable.

**Progress signal**: "Ready for community water tasks" with hand-off readiness score (e.g., 80%+ independence, consistent quality, good judgment on safety). User can now assist/evaluate lower-level apprentices under oversight.

- **After every task**: Ask: "What did you do? What went wrong? Show result (photos of setup, connections, water tests, measurements, before/after). Where did you hesitate?"
- Then: Pinpoint mistakes → minimal explanation of root cause → assign focused correction (e.g., "Re-seal these 3 connections with proper technique").
- **Progress tracking**: Completed tasks, repeatability (multiple leak-free runs), independence (help requested?), diagnostic accuracy, real-world impact (gallons captured, repairs lasting).
- **Advancement**: Strict gates — only advance on verified results. Loop back with variations if quality slips.
- **Real-world push**: Every Level 3+ task must deliver community/home value (storage for emergencies, irrigation to grow food, repairs to shared systems).
- **Failure library potential**: Anonymized logs of issues (e.g., "leaks from overtightened PVC" or "algae from unsanitized barrel").
- **Scalability notes**: Start simple (no electricity/pumps). Later expansions could include basic pumps, advanced filtration, or well maintenance. Always stress testing water before drinking.

This track is concrete, verifiable via photos/descriptions/tests, and directly convertible to reliable skills. It avoids theory dumps — every step centers on doing useful water work safely.

## Sanitation Track

**Goal**: Turn a complete beginner into a reliable operator who can safely manage human waste (blackwater), greywater, personal hygiene, trash, and basic cleaning in home or community settings — especially during grid-down, off-grid, or emergency scenarios. Prevents disease, contamination, and odor while creating usable outputs (e.g., compost, irrigation water). Integrates tightly with the Water Systems track (greywater reuse, clean water for hygiene).

**Critical Safety & Hygiene Note** (enforced by the copilot at every step): Sanitation work involves pathogens. Always use gloves, eye protection, dedicated tools, and handwashing protocols. Separate clean vs. dirty workflows. Never contaminate potable water sources. In real emergencies, prioritize containment to avoid spreading illness. Local regulations may apply for composting or greywater systems.

Before any waste handling:

- Identify and list tools/materials: gloves (nitrile/latex), safety glasses, dedicated buckets (5-gallon with lids, marked #1 urine / #2 feces), heavy-duty trash bags, sawdust/wood shavings/peat moss/carbon material, shovel/trowel, hand sanitizer or soap, bleach/unscented chlorine for disinfection, measuring tools.
- **Mandatory setup**: Designated "dirty" workspace away from food/water areas. Handwashing station with soap and clean water. Understand basic contamination risks (fecal-oral route, flies, groundwater).
- **Task**: Describe your current sanitation setup (existing toilet, waste disposal method) and list available supplies. Send photo of workspace, safety gear, and labeled buckets.
- **Feedback**: Correct missing safety/hygiene items immediately. Assign reinforcement (e.g., practice proper handwashing technique for 20+ seconds) if needed.

**Progress signal**: Safe, hygienic setup + contamination awareness demonstrated → Unlock Level 1.

## Level 1 – Basic Actions (Tool Familiarity, Materials, Safe Handling)

Focus: Safe separation of waste streams, basic containment, personal hygiene, and material identification. Build habits that prevent immediate health risks.

**Tasks** (in order, repeat for consistency):

1. **Set Up and Use Separate Urine/Feces Collection (Bucket Toilet Basics)**
   - Task: Prepare two 5-gallon buckets (one for urine/#1, one for feces/#2 lined with heavy-duty bag). Practice depositing waste correctly (urine separate, feces + toilet paper covered with carbon material like sawdust). Do this for 3–5 uses.
   - Success criteria: Proper separation maintained, no spills or cross-contamination, odor controlled with cover material.
   - Just-in-time: Why separate urine (sterile, reduces volume/odor) vs. feces (pathogen-heavy); cover ratio (1–2 cups carbon per use).
   - Feedback: "What did you do? Any odor/spillage/hesitation? Send photos of setup, cover layer, and sealed buckets." Common errors: Mixing streams, insufficient cover, poor sealing → targeted correction (e.g., remake cover layer on 3 new deposits).

2. **Identify & Prepare Carbon Cover Materials**
   - Task: Source, sort, and prepare 10+ cups of cover material (sawdust, wood shavings, shredded paper, peat). Test absorption/odor control on a small fecal simulant (or safe substitute).
   - Success: Correct identification of effective carbon sources; material ready for use without dust or contamination.
   - Error use: Poor absorption? Teach why high-carbon, low-nitrogen materials work for composting.

3. **Basic Handwashing & Surface Disinfection**
   - Task: Demonstrate proper handwashing (20+ seconds with soap) after simulated waste handling. Disinfect a small surface or tool with diluted bleach solution (proper ratio).
   - Success: Technique verified (timer + description), surface visibly clean and no residue.

**Progress gate to Level 2**: 3–5 successful separate waste deposits with proper covering + repeatable handwashing/disinfection. Verified via photos/descriptions. Copilot: "Ready for controlled systems" or "Reinforce separation/odor control."
**Repeatability check**: User repeats a similar containment task without prompting.

## Level 2 – Controlled Builds (Small, Defined Assemblies – Repeat for Consistency)

Focus: Assemble simple, functional sanitation components. Emphasize containment, odor/vector control, and basic processing.

**Tasks**:

1. **Build a Basic Sawdust Bucket Composting Toilet System**
   - Task: Set up a functional bucket toilet station with seat (or riser), urine diverter if available, cover material dispenser, and ventilation/odor control (e.g., simple vent pipe or charcoal filter). Use for multiple deposits and manage the pile (add carbon, monitor moisture).
   - Materials: Buckets, lid/seat, carbon supply, optional diverter.
   - Success: System contains waste without leaks/odors/flies for several days; pile stays aerobic (not soggy). Test by adding cover and checking after 24–48 hours.
   - Feedback: Multiple-angle photos + description of use and checks. "What went wrong?"
   - Common errors: Anaerobic pile (too wet → bad smell), insufficient carbon, poor sealing → corrections on moisture balance and cover technique.

2. **Repeat with Variations**
   - Add a second bucket in rotation or build a simple greywater collection bucket for handwashing/sink runoff. Goal: Consistent results with less guidance.

**Progress gate**: 2–3 successful operational periods where the system functions hygienically (no complaints of odor/containment issues). User explains adjustments made.
**Just-in-time**: Basic composting principles (carbon:nitrogen balance, aeration, temperature); greywater vs. blackwater separation.

## Level 3 – Real-World Tasks (Fix or Build Something Needed)

Focus: Apply skills to actual home/community sanitation needs. Immediate value in hygiene and waste management.

**Tasks** (copilot assigns based on user environment/resources):

1. **Repair or Improve an Existing Sanitation Issue**
   - Examples: Fix a leaking portable toilet seal, address odor in current setup, unclog/improve a greywater drain, or upgrade a standard toilet for low-water use (e.g., dual-flush or bucket flush method).
   - Task: Diagnose, repair, test for containment/odor/hygiene.
   - Success: Issue resolved, system performs better/safer. Before/after photos + function test (e.g., no leaks, reduced odor).

2. **Build/Install a Useful Sanitation Feature**
   - Examples: Full rotating bucket composting system for household use; simple greywater diversion from sink/shower to garden (with basic filter); emergency latrine setup (e.g., cathole technique or trench for short-term outdoor use, 200+ ft from water); trash sorting/compaction station to reduce waste volume.
   - Success: System handles daily waste effectively, integrates safely (no contamination of water sources), provides measurable benefit (e.g., compost starting, greywater reused for irrigation, trash volume reduced).
   - Real integration: "This will support your household or community during water shortages — measure daily waste volume first."

**Progress gate**: At least one repair + one new build completed and verified in use. User reports real outcome (e.g., "Handled 5 days of waste with no odor; greywater irrigated X plants").
**Just-in-time** (as needed): Safe cathole/trench digging (depth, location, covering); greywater safety (avoid kitchen/blackwater initially, basic filtration).

## Level 4 – Diagnosis (Identify Problems, Choose Solutions)

Focus: Troubleshooting and judgment for complex or failing systems.

**Tasks**:

1. **Analyze and Fix a Problematic Sanitation Setup**
   - Task: Identify an issue (odor, flies, slow composting, contamination signs, overflowing greywater, or backup in existing plumbing). Diagnose root cause (insufficient carbon? Wrong location? Overloading? Poor separation?). Execute fix.
   - Success: Accurate diagnosis + lasting resolution. Explain alternatives (e.g., more aeration vs. additives) and trade-offs.
   - Feedback: Detailed before/during/after description + photos. Probe: "What failed and why? What other solutions exist?"

2. **Plan a Multi-Component System with Risk Assessment**
   - Example: Design an integrated household sanitation plan (toilet system + greywater reuse + trash management + hygiene station) for a specific scenario (e.g., 4-person household off-grid for 30+ days). Identify failure points in advance (freezing, vectors, overload). Build/test a portion.

**Progress gate**: Consistent accurate diagnosis and independent fixes. Copilot flags "Needs reinforcement in pathogen control" or similar.
**Error-first**: Common real-world mistakes (mixing urine/feces, insufficient cover leading to flies/pathogens, planting over leach fields if septic, flushing non-approved items) become targeted teaching moments using anonymized past examples.

## Level 5 – Independence (Plan, Execute, Adjust Without Guidance)

Focus: User leads real projects. Copilot shifts to reviewer/consultant.

**Tasks**:

- User proposes a practical community/home sanitation project (e.g., full composting toilet system for shared facility, greywater irrigation network for garden/food production, emergency sanitation plan for group shelter, or long-term humanure composting setup that produces usable soil amendment).
- Requirements: Detailed plan (materials list, steps, safety/contamination controls, capacity estimates, maintenance schedule), risk assessment (disease vectors, environmental impact).
- Execute, document process/outcomes, adjust for real issues (e.g., material shortages, weather, higher-than-expected volume).
- Success: Project delivers reliable, hygienic waste management with measurable impact (e.g., waste processed safely, resources reused), minimal copilot input, and maintainable long-term.

**Progress signal**: "Ready for community sanitation tasks" with hand-off readiness score (e.g., 80%+ independence, strong judgment on safety/hygiene). User can now assist/evaluate lower-level apprentices under oversight.

- **After every task**: Standard questions — "What did you do? What went wrong? Show result (photos of setups, waste layers, before/after, tests for odor/containment/leaks). Where did you hesitate?"
- Then: Pinpoint specific mistakes (e.g., "Pile too wet due to insufficient carbon") → minimal root-cause explanation → assign focused correction task.
- **Progress tracking**: Tasks completed, repeatability (multiple hygienic cycles), independence (help requested?), diagnostic accuracy, real-world impact (waste managed safely, resources recovered).
- **Advancement**: Strict gates — only advance on verified, safe results. Loop back with variations if hygiene/containment slips.
- **Real-world push**: Every Level 3+ task must deliver immediate community/home value (safer living conditions, reduced disease risk, compost for food production, greywater for irrigation).
- **Failure library potential**: Anonymized logs of issues (e.g., "flies from uncovered pile" or "odor from anaerobic conditions") for future users.
- **Integration notes**: Links naturally to Water Systems (greywater handling, clean water for washing) and Builder (constructing toilet structures, shelters, or stands). Start simple/no electricity. Expansions could include basic septic troubleshooting or advanced composting monitoring.

This track is concrete, verifiable primarily via photos/descriptions/odor/function reports, and centers entirely on doing useful, safe sanitation work. It avoids theory dumps and directly addresses the "S" in SHTF that many systems ignore.

It produces sanitation operators others can rely on for critical health protection in any scenario.

# Food Production Track

**Goal**: Turn a beginner into a capable operator who can reliably grow, harvest, and manage edible crops for household or community needs. Emphasis on soil health, season extension, pest management, and integration with other tracks. Start with low-tech, small-scale methods (containers, raised beds).

**Critical Safety & Hygiene Note**: Use clean water for irrigation. Avoid contamination from sanitation/compost. Wear gloves for soil work. Identify garden hazards (sharp tools, insects, toxic plants). Wash produce thoroughly.

## Prerequisites (Safety & Setup – Do This First)

- Identify tools/materials: shovel, trowel, hoe, watering can/hose, pH/soil test kit (or jar test), seeds/starts, compost or soil amendments, mulch, gloves, pruning shears, stakes/string.
- Assess site: sunlight (6+ hours ideal), drainage, soil type, water access, potential contaminants.
- **Task**: Map your available growing space. Test soil or container medium. List accessible seeds/tools. Send photo of site + setup.
- **Feedback**: Correct issues (poor drainage, contaminated area) immediately. Reinforce safety (tool handling, handwashing after soil work).

**Progress signal**: Site assessed + basic setup ready → Unlock Level 1.

## Level 1 – Basic Actions (Tool Familiarity, Soil/Materials, Simple Planting)

Focus: Understand soil, seeds, watering, and basic plant needs. Build habits for healthy starts.

**Tasks** (in order, repeat for consistency):

1. **Soil Preparation & Testing**
   - Task: Prepare 3–5 small containers or a small bed. Test soil (texture, pH if possible) or mix potting medium. Amend with compost.
   - Success: Loose, workable soil; proper drainage test (water drains in <1 hour).
   - Just-in-time: Soil components (sand/silt/clay), why amendments matter, avoiding compaction.
   - Feedback: Photos of prepared medium + drainage test. Errors (heavy clay, poor drainage) → correction (add more organic matter).

2. **Seed Starting & Planting Basics**
   - Task: Sow 3 easy crops (lettuce, radish, beans) at correct depth/spacing. Label clearly. Water properly.
   - Success: Even germination, no damping-off (overwatering).

3. **Basic Watering & Observation**

# Primitive Skills & Bushcraft Track

**Goal**: Synthesize and deepen many prior skills in raw, no-electricity, scavenged-resource scenarios. Teaches how to thrive with minimal manufactured goods using natural materials for shelter, tools, fire, cordage, traps, and basic containers. Makes the entire system “primitive-proof” by providing ultimate adaptability and fallback capability when modern supplies are limited or unavailable.

**Critical Safety Note** (enforced throughout): Primitive skills involve knives, fire, falls, and wild plants/animals. Prioritize safety, start small, and link to Medic for injuries/infections. Ethical harvesting only. Know your limits — practice in controlled settings before real need.

## Prerequisites (Safety & Setup – Do This First)

- Identify and inventory: knife (fixed blade), cordage/paracord, fire starters (as backup), notebook, basic pack with water/Medic items.
- **Task**: Assess a safe practice area (backyard/nearby woods). List natural materials available locally. Review key risks (exposure, injury, misID). Send description/sketch of practice site + gear.
- **Feedback**: Correct unsafe practices or gaps immediately.

**Progress signal**: Safe mindset + basic kit readiness → Unlock Level 1.

## Level 1 – Basic Actions (Fire, Cordage, Simple Tools)

3. **Natural Containers & Baskets**
   - Task: Weave or carve basic vessels (bark container, woven basket) for water/food storage.

# Advanced Medical / Herbalism Track

Emphasizes prevention, safe preparation, and strict limits.

**Critical Safety & Legal Note** (enforced throughout): This is **not** a substitute for professional medical care or licensing. Know your limits — stabilize and seek higher help when possible. Many herbs have interactions or toxicities; use only well-identified, tested applications. Link heavily to Medic (Basic), Foraging, and Sanitation. Document everything. In real scenarios, prioritize evacuation for serious conditions.

## Prerequisites (Safety & Setup – Do This First)
