# Parent Implementation Guide

**Builder Program – Year 1: Foundations of Self-Reliance**

This guide is written specifically for you — the parent or lead educator — to help you successfully implement the curriculum with your neurodivergent student.

### Mindset Shift

This is **not** a traditional “sit down and do worksheets” curriculum.
It is a **real-world life skills and self-reliance program**.
Your role is more coach and facilitator than traditional teacher.

### Best Practices for Success

1. **Start with Week 0**
   Do not skip the onboarding week. It sets safety expectations, introduces the Builder Journal, and creates buy-in from your student.

2. **Keep It Flexible**
   - One main project per week is plenty.
   - It’s okay to spend 2 weeks on a skill if your student is really engaged.
   - It’s also okay to skip or shorten a week if your family is overwhelmed.

3. **Use the Difficulty Tiers**
   Let your student help choose their level (Explorer / Builder / Pathfinder) for each skill. Giving them choice increases motivation and reduces resistance.

4. **Incorporate Neurodivergent Supports Daily**
   - Build in regulation breaks
   - Offer visual schedules or checklists
   - Allow movement while working
   - Use “First / Then” language
   - Celebrate effort and small wins generously

5. **Make It Family-Friendly**
   - Involve siblings when possible (even in small roles)
   - Turn big projects into family events
   - Use the skills in real daily life (cooking, cleaning, gardening, etc.)

6. **Documentation & Reflection**
   - Encourage your student to use the **Builder Journal** (even if it’s just drawings or photos at first)
   - Take progress photos — they become very motivating over time
   - At the end of each quarter, do a short family review

### Common Challenges & Solutions

| Challenge                        | Suggested Solution                              |
|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Student loses interest           | Give more choice or switch to a related project |
| Overwhelm / resistance           | Slow down, add more regulation breaks          |
| You feel behind                  | Remember: progress over perfection             |
| Safety concerns                  | Always do safety review first                  |
| Siblings want to join            | Assign them helpful roles                      |

### How Often to Use This Program

- **Ideal:** 2–4 hours per week (spread out)
- **Minimum viable:** 1 focused project per week
- **Maximum:** Integrate skills into daily life as much as possible

You do **not** need to finish every single skill in exactly 36 weeks.
The goal is steady growth, confidence, and real capability — not completion.

You are doing meaningful, important work.
Your student is learning skills that will serve them for the rest of their life.

Thank you for being their guide.
