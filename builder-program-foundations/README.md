# Builder Program – Foundations of Self-Reliance

**Year 1: Foundations of Self-Reliance**
Real-world life skills and self-reliance education for modern homeschooling families with neurodivergent students.

**Duration:** 36 weeks (1 full school year)
**Format:** Project-based, flexible, neurodivergent-friendly
**Ages:** 8–14 (with built-in difficulty tiers)

### Core Design Principles (Built In)

- Safety First (explicit every week)
- Neurodivergent Accessibility Layer (adaptations, regulation breaks, choice-based paths)
- Reflection & Documentation System (Builder Journal + progress tracking)
- Seasonal Alignment (where logical)
- Family & Community Integration
- Emotional & Ethical Framework (stewardship, calm preparedness, compassion)
- Economics & Trade
- Digital Preparedness
- Capstone Project

### Difficulty Tiers (for every skill)

- **Explorer Tier** (ages 8–10): High support, simpler tasks
- **Builder Tier** (ages 11–12): Moderate independence
- **Pathfinder Tier** (ages 13–14): Advanced, leadership-focused

### Week 0: Builder Foundations (Onboarding Week)

- Safety Frameworks (tool, fire, water, food, outdoor, animal, herbalism, emergency protocols)
- Builder Mindset & Emotional Framework
- Create personal Builder Journal
- Set up regulation plan + “Ask an adult first” rules
- Introduce difficulty tiers and choice-based learning
- Family roles and expectations

### Weekly Template (Used Every Week)

Every week includes:

- Core Skill
- Hands-On Project
- Safety Notes
- Neurodivergent Adaptations / Regulation Breaks
- Reflection Prompt (Builder Journal)
- Real-World Application
- Family / Community Integration Idea
- Optional Advanced Challenge

### Full 36-Week Outline

**Quarter 1: Water, Food & Daily Needs** (Weeks 1–9)
Theme: Meeting basic human needs

- Week 1: Water Systems + Safety Frameworks
- Week 2: Sanitation & Hygiene
- Week 3: Food Production (soil & growing)
- Week 4: Food Preservation & Storage
- Week 5: Kitchen Safety + Basic Cooking
- Week 6: Economics of Food (budgeting, needs vs wants)
- Week 7: Digital Tools for Tracking Resources
- Week 8: Reflection & Family Integration
- Week 9: Quarter 1 Capstone Project + Review

**Quarter 2: Home Systems & Maintenance** (Weeks 10–18)
Theme: Building and maintaining a functional home

- Week 10: Repair & Maintenance
- Week 11: Tool Making & Basic Fabrication
- Week 12: Energy, Fire & Lighting (safety heavy)
- Week 13: Inventory & Resource Management
- Week 14: Economics & Trade Basics
- Week 15: Digital Preparedness (offline storage, power banks)
- Week 16: Mental Resilience & Decision-Making
- Week 17: Family Project Day
- Week 18: Quarter 2 Review & Reflection

**Quarter 3: People, Communication & Leadership** (Weeks 19–27)
Theme: Leading yourself and others

- Week 19: Medic (Basic) + Herbalism Safety
- Week 20: Communication & Signaling
- Week 21: Emotional & Ethical Framework
- Week 22: Leadership & Group Coordination
- Week 23: Mental Resilience under stress
- Week 24: Neurodivergent Communication Styles
- Week 25: Community Service / Helping Others
- Week 26: Family Integration Project
- Week 27: Quarter 3 Review

**Quarter 4: Outdoor Self-Reliance & Advanced Skills** (Weeks 28–36)
Theme: Living with and from the land (seasonally aligned where possible)

- Week 28: Navigation & Mapping
- Week 29: Foraging & Wild Food Procurement
- Week 30: Primitive Skills & Bushcraft
- Week 31: Animal Husbandry + Beekeeping
- Week 32: Security & Perimeter Basics
- Week 33: Advanced Medical / Herbalism
- Week 34: Economics of Self-Reliance (bartering, value)
- Week 35: Full Capstone Project Planning
- Week 36: Builder Capstone Showcase + Year-End Reflection & Portfolio

### Final Capstone Project (Week 35–36)

**“Build Your Mini Self-Reliance Station”**
Students design, plan, budget, and present a small functional station that demonstrates multiple skills learned throughout the year.

This outline is complete, balanced, neurodivergent-friendly, seasonally aware, and includes safety, reflection, economics, digital skills, ethics, and family integration.
