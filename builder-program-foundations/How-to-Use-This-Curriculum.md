# How to Use This Curriculum

**Builder Program – Year 1: Foundations of Self-Reliance**

Welcome! This guide will help you get the most out of the Builder Program with your student.

### Who This Curriculum Is For

- Homeschooling families with neurodivergent students (ages 8–14)
- Parents who want practical, real-world life skills instead of traditional worksheets
- Families looking for hands-on projects that build confidence and independence

### Getting Started (Week 0 – Builder Foundations)

Before you begin any skills:

1. Complete **Week 0: Builder Foundations** (onboarding week)
2. Read the **Safety Frameworks** together
3. Create your student’s **Builder Journal**
4. Set up a simple regulation plan and “Ask an adult first” rule
5. Discuss the three difficulty tiers (Explorer, Builder, Pathfinder) and let your student help choose their starting level

### Weekly Rhythm (Recommended Flow)

Each week follows this simple pattern:

- **Core Skill** – Learn the main topic
- **Hands-On Project** – Build or practice something real
- **Safety Notes** – Review relevant safety rules
- **Neurodivergent Adaptations** – Choose supports that work for your student
- **Reflection Prompt** – Write or draw in the Builder Journal
- **Real-World Application** – Use the skill in daily life
- **Family / Community Integration** – Involve others when possible
- **Optional Advanced Challenge** – For students who want more

### How to Adapt for Your Student

- Use the **Explorer / Builder / Pathfinder** tiers to adjust difficulty
- Offer **choice** whenever possible (which project, which order, how to document)
- Build in **regulation breaks** and movement as needed
- Use visual schedules and checklists from your other HomeschoolGuide resources
- Celebrate small wins generously

### Progress Tracking

- Use the **Builder Journal** every week
- Take photos of projects for a visual portfolio
- Complete the **Progress Tracker** at the end of each quarter
- Update the student’s **Getting to Know Me Profile** as they grow

### Tips for Success

- Start small — one project per week is plenty
- Focus on **progress over perfection**
- Involve siblings or the whole family when it makes sense
- Use the **Transition Support Toolkit** during week-to-week changes
- Keep a relaxed pace — it’s okay to stretch a week into two

### How This Fits Into Your Homeschool

You do **not** need to follow this curriculum rigidly.
Treat it as a flexible menu of real-life skills. Mix and match with your existing homeschool rhythm.

The goal is not to finish every skill in exactly 36 weeks — it’s to help your student become more capable, confident, and self-reliant in the real world.

You’ve got this. Your student is lucky to have you.
